About Mangalore Temples

Mangalore, a coastal city in Karnataka, India, is known for its rich cultural heritage and beautiful temples. These temples are not only places of worship but also architectural marvels that attract tourists from all over the world. Here are some of the most famous temples in Mangalore that you should visit.

Kudroli Gokarnath Temple

Kudroli Gokarnath Temple is a beautiful temple in Mangalore dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Kudroli Temple

Kudroli Gokarnath Temple is one of the most prominent temples in Mangalore. It was built in 1912 by Narayana Guru and has since been a major center of worship. The temple is known for its grand celebrations of Navaratri and attracts thousands of devotees during this time.

Mangaladevi Temple

Mangaladevi Temple is an ancient temple in Mangalore dedicated to Goddess Mangaladevi.

Mangaladevi Temple

Mangaladevi Temple, built in the 9th century, is a significant historical and religious site. The temple is named after Goddess Mangaladevi and is believed to be the origin of the name of the city, Mangalore. The temple is particularly famous for its Navaratri festival, which draws pilgrims from far and wide.

Kateel Durgaparameshwari Temple

Kateel Durgaparameshwari Temple is located on an islet in the Nandini River and is dedicated to Goddess Durga Parameshwari.

Kateel Temple

Kateel Durgaparameshwari Temple is one of the most renowned temples in Karnataka. Situated on an islet in the Nandini River, it is dedicated to Goddess Durga Parameshwari. The temple is known for its serene environment and vibrant festivals, particularly Durga Puja and Rathotsava.
