About Mangalore Temples

Mangalore, a coastal city in Karnataka, India, is known for its rich cultural heritage and beautiful temples. These temples are not only places of worship but also architectural marvels that attract tourists from all over the world. Here are some of the most famous temples in Mangalore that you should visit.

Kudroli Gokarnath Temple

Kudroli Gokarnath Temple is a beautiful temple in Mangalore dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Kudroli Temple

History

Kudroli Gokarnath Temple was established in 1912 by Narayana Guru. It has become a major center for worship and a hub for religious activities.

Festivals & Events

The temple is renowned for its Navaratri celebrations, which are attended by thousands of devotees. Other major events include the annual temple festival and the Rathotsava (chariot festival).

Mangaladevi Temple

Mangaladevi Temple is an ancient temple in Mangalore dedicated to Goddess Mangaladevi.

View on Google Maps

Mangaladevi Temple

History

Mangaladevi Temple, built in the 9th century, is a significant historical and religious site. The temple is named after Goddess Mangaladevi and is believed to be the origin of the name of the city, Mangalore.

Festivals & Events

The temple is particularly famous for its Navaratri festival, which draws pilgrims from far and wide. The annual festival sees a grand celebration with traditional dances and processions.

Kateel Temple

History

Kateel Durgaparameshwari Temple is renowned for its location on an islet in the Nandini River. It is a prominent pilgrimage site and is dedicated to Goddess Durga Parameshwari.

Festivals & Events

The temple is famous for its annual Durga Puja and Rathotsava (chariot festival). These events attract thousands of devotees and are celebrated with grandeur.
